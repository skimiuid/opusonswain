Meet Your Mentors

Musicians dedicated to the art of teaching.

Ding Zhou

Co-Founder & Instructor

Ding Zhou is a pianist celebrated for his "poetic touch and profound emotional depth." A graduate of the prestigious Jacobs School of Music, Ding has dedicated his career to the art of the piano, specializing in the Classical repertoire and Baroque piano literature.

As an educator, Ding believes that technique is merely a tool to unlock imagination. His teaching method combines the virtuosity with a modern, anatomical approach to movement, ensuring students play with freedom and avoid injury.

Specialties: Baroque, Classical, Technical Fundation, Competition Preparation.

Miles Swaminathan

Co-Founder & Instructor

Miles Swaminathan is a pianist and composer at the Indiana University Jacobs School of Music, where he studies under Jean-Louis Haguenauer. A recipient of the 2024 Florence Price Piano Concerto Award and the Artist Presentation Society Piano Prize, he has also been recognized as a laureate of the Music International Grand Prix for Original Composition. In 2025, his solo work A Night at the Observatory won First Prize in the Tonal Category at the 18th Fidelio International Piano Competition. Equally devoted to performance and creation, Swaminathan continues to shape a distinctive artistic voice that bridges virtuosity, lyricism, and imagination.
